# Purpose

This document captures campaign learnings, quality standards and optimization rules identified during testing and real campaign execution.

All future agents should use these rules to improve output quality over time.

---

# General Principles

## Standardization Before Automation

The first goal is not full automation.

The first goal is a standardized and repeatable campaign process.

Automation should only be increased once quality and consistency have been validated.

---

# Digital Media Brand Principles

The Digital Media Brand (Alex) is not a thought leader, influencer or personal brand.

Alex acts as a communication layer for CONVOTIS expertise.

Alex may communicate:

* Observations
* Perspectives
* Industry developments
* Customer realities
* Expert insights

Alex should never invent expertise.

All expertise must originate from identified subject matter experts.

The audience should perceive Alex as a trusted industry communicator rather than a content creator.

---


## Human Review Remains Essential

All generated outputs should be reviewed before execution.

The objective is to reduce manual effort, not eliminate human judgment.

---

## Execution Over Creativity

The objective is not to create impressive AI output.

The objective is to create useful campaign assets that can be executed with minimal effort.

---

# Digital Media Brand Content Rules

## Observation Over Explanation

Thought Leadership should feel like:

- A customer conversation
- A workshop observation
- A project experience
- A market observation
- A real-world situation

Avoid:

- Educational articles
- Blog-style content
- Generic business advice
- Framework explanations
- LinkedIn creator content

The best content feels like:

A relevant observation.

A practical insight.

A business reality worth considering.

Not:

An educational article.

A LinkedIn creator post.

A generic business lesson.


## Expert Validation

Every Digital Media Brand asset should be linked to at least one identified expert knowledge source.

Alex communicates expertise.

Experts provide expertise.

The source of expertise should always be traceable within the campaign strategy.

---

## Practitioner First

Write like:

- A consultant
- A subject matter expert
- A practitioner

Alex may communicate expert perspectives, but only when they are grounded in expert knowledge provided by CONVOTIS specialists.

Do not write like:

- A marketer
- A copywriter
- A LinkedIn influencer
- A content creator

---

## Direct Audience Language

Direct audience language is encouraged.

Examples:

- Wenn Sie aktuell für Informationssicherheit verantwortlich sind ...
- Falls Ihr Team bereits ausgelastet ist ...
- Wenn Sie gerade vor einer NIS2-Bewertung stehen ...

---

## Community Engagement

Use community questions sparingly.

Maximum:

30% of posts.

Examples:

- Wie erleben Sie das aktuell?
- Welche Erfahrungen haben Sie gemacht?
- Ist das bei Ihnen ähnlich?

Never use engagement bait.

---

# AI Writing Blacklist

Never use:

- Das ist kein ...
- Das ist ...
- Nicht ...
- Sondern ...
- Nicht weil ...
- Sondern weil ...
- Das Problem war nicht ...
- Das Problem war ...
- Es geht nicht um ...
- Es geht um ...
- Viele Unternehmen ...
- Die meisten Unternehmen ...
- In meiner Erfahrung ...
- Was mich häufig beschäftigt ...
- Der erste Schritt ...
- Transparenz ist der erste Schritt ...
- Alles andere kommt danach ...

Avoid all artificial contrast structures.

Avoid consultant clichés.

Avoid LinkedIn creator language.

Never write like:

- A LinkedIn influencer
- A personal brand
- A motivational creator
- A social media personality

---

# Writing Style Rules

Use:

- Natural sentence flow
- Mixed sentence lengths
- Practical language
- Real observations
- Human phrasing

Avoid:

- Marketing buzzwords
- Over-polished language
- Corporate jargon
- Generic AI phrasing

---

# Storytelling Rules

Stories should feel remembered.

Not written.

Prefer:

- Workshop moments
- Customer conversations
- Delivery experiences
- Real project situations

Avoid:

- Fictional stories
- Invented customer examples
- Artificial anecdotes

---

# Language Rules

## German Quotation Marks

Always use:

„Text“

Never use:

'Text'

or

"Text"

---

## Dashes

Minimize use of dashes.

Target:

Maximum 1 dash per 300 words.

---

## Conversion

Always use:

Conversion

Never use:

Konversion

# Visual Content Rules

## Authenticity First

Images should feel:

- Natural
- Authentic
- Human
- Documentary

Not:

- Corporate
- Perfect
- Overproduced

---

## Preferred Style

Use:

- Smartphone photography
- Natural lighting
- Real office environments
- Workshop situations
- Customer meetings
- Whiteboard discussions

---

## Authentic Image Prompt Rules

Images should feel accidental.

Preferred:

- Slightly imperfect framing
- Motion blur
- Uneven lighting
- Cluttered desks
- People looking away from camera
- Unstaged moments

Avoid:

- Perfect composition
- Professional photography
- Studio environments
- Obvious posing

Goal:

The image should look like somebody casually took a photo during a normal workday.

---

# Digital Media Brand Visual Rules

If the Digital Media Brand (Alex) is used, the same authenticity principles apply.

Alex should appear as a professional industry communicator rather than a thought leader, influencer or content creator.

Preferred:

- Natural business environments
- Manufacturing environments
- Office environments
- Workshop situations
- Conference environments
- Customer-facing situations

Alex should feel like part of the environment.

The focus should remain on the business topic rather than on Alex as a person.

Avoid:

- Influencer aesthetics
- Creator-style content
- Luxury lifestyle imagery
- Personal branding photography
- Excessive focus on Alex


Alex is a communication layer for expertise.

The expertise and business topic should always remain the primary focus.

---

# Creative Production Rules

For every visual asset provide:

1. Canva Creation Guidance
2. AI Image Generation Prompt

The Canva version remains the preferred production method.

The AI Image Prompt serves as an alternative production workflow and future automation path.

If AI-generated visuals are used, they must follow the Authentic Image Prompt Rules and Digital Media Brand Visual Rules.

---

# LinkedIn Paid Asset Rules

## Single Image Ads

The visual message is the asset.

The primary text is secondary.

Preferred:

- Short statements
- Strong observations
- Clear business reality
- Minimalistic design

Avoid:

- Long headlines
- Complex graphics
- Multiple messages
- Generic marketing claims

Always provide:

- Visual Text Option A
- Visual Text Option B
- Canva Creation Guidance
- AI Creative Prompt

The Canva version remains the preferred production method.

AI-generated creative prompts should be provided as an alternative production path.

---

## Canva Principle

Assume ads are created inside predefined Canva templates.

Generate:

- Visual Text
- Supporting Copy
- Design Notes
- Canva Creation Guidance
- AI Creative Prompt

The Canva version remains the preferred production method.

The AI Creative Prompt serves as an alternative production path.

---

# Document Ad Rules

Document Ads should not feel like mini whitepapers.

Preferred:

- 3–6 slides
- One idea per slide
- Fast reading
- Story-driven structure

Use:

- Customer situations
- Market observations
- Practical experiences

Avoid:

- Long explanations
- Framework-heavy content
- Educational documents

Document Ads may be produced:

- Manually in Canva
- Through future AI creative workflows

Therefore concise slide copy should always be provided.

# Outbound Rules

## LinkedIn Connection Request

Preferred:

75–200 characters

Maximum:

250 characters

---

## LinkedIn Follow-Up

Maximum:

500 characters

---

## Email Outreach

Preferred:

50–100 words

Maximum:

120 words

Emails should:

- Get to the point quickly
- Be easy to answer
- Be highly relevant

Avoid:

- Long introductions
- Generic context
- Marketing language

# Campaign Operations Rules

## Naming Convention

All assets must follow the campaign naming convention.

Asset names should always contain the campaign identifier.

---

## Tracking Structure

Generate naming for:

- LinkedIn Campaigns
- HubSpot Campaigns

- Lists
- Workflows
- Emails
- UTM Parameters

All naming should be consistent and reusable.
